Why Orbital Risk Demands Action

Kessler Syndrome describes a cascading collision process where debris generates more debris, making critical orbits increasingly unsafe for satellites and future missions.

Sustainability in orbit is no longer optional. Delayed mitigation can lock policy-makers into expensive and reactive responses instead of proactive, coordinated prevention.

AstraForecast is designed to support proactive policy planning by quantifying how intervention timing influences long-horizon debris outcomes.

Data Transparency

  • Source: Project dataset file `datasets/space_launches.csv` (curated historical series).
  • Coverage: 2000 through the latest dataset year.
  • Preprocessing: year sorting, schema filtering to launch/satellite columns, cumulative derivations.

Why Polynomial Instead of Exponential

Exponential fits can overstate long-run growth when data is limited and policy interventions are expected. A degree-2 polynomial provides a flexible but more conservative trend surface for policy-support forecasting.
